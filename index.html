    How Chinese words are built

    Most Chinese words are made of two characters that snap together like LEGO bricks. There are five classic ways they combine — once you see the patterns, thousands of words suddenly make sense. Tap 🎲 for fresh examples, tap a word to open it in the dictionary.

    Tone trainer

    Tones are the hardest part of Chinese. Listen to a character and pick its tone. The four tones: 1 stays high and flat, 2 rises, 3 dips down then up, 4 falls sharply.

    About

    写字 Xiězì is a free, offline-first Chinese learning app that runs entirely in your browser. There is no account, no server and no tracking — nothing you write, say or study leaves your device, and your progress is stored locally (in this browser only).

    Privacy — microphone & speech

    Speaking practice and voice input use your browser's built-in speech recognition. On iPhone/Safari this runs on-device. On Chrome/Android the browser may send the recorded audio to the browser maker's servers (e.g. Google) to transcribe it — this is handled by the browser, not by this app. The microphone is used only while you hold a speaking exercise, and nothing is recorded or stored by the app.
